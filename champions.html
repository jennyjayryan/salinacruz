New Zealand’s Greatest Achievement

Jacko
Grand Champion

NZ Grand Ch Salinacruz Jack Flash

In the history of New Zealand Chihuahua showing, no dog has climbed higher than Jacko. As New Zealand’s first — and to this day only — Grand Champion Chihuahua, Jacko achieved what no dog of the breed had done before him.

The Grand Championship title in New Zealand requires a dog to first become a Champion and then accumulate Challenge Certificates at an elite level far beyond what is required for the standard title. It is a title that demands extraordinary quality, consistency, and longevity in the ring.

Bred by Yvonne Tester and proudly carrying the Salinacruz name, Jacko stands as the pinnacle of what the kennel has produced — and as a milestone in New Zealand show dog history.

Beyond New Zealand

International Champions

Salinacruz dogs have earned championship titles in four countries — a testament to the universal quality the kennel produces.

🇦🇺

Australia

Salinacruz exports have achieved Australian championship titles across the Tasman. The quality that wins in New Zealand travels well.

🇺🇸

United States

Otto holds both an American Championship and a New Zealand Championship — a rare dual-country title holder and a mark of genuine international class.

🇿🇦

South Africa

Dogs bred at Salinacruz have travelled to South Africa and been successful in that country’s show rings, adding another continent to the kennel’s map.
